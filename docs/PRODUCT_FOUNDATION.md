# Product Foundation

## Brand Language

### Canonical Relationship

Sivraj builds and protects your sovereign AI Twin.

- **Sivraj** is the product, platform, API, app, encryption/storage system, and coordination layer.
- **Your Twin** is the user-owned context and intelligence Sivraj builds from your memories, documents, goals, preferences, graph, and permissions.
- **Sovereign AI Twin** is the formal concept used in positioning, onboarding, and product education.
- **Sovereign** is the mission: user-owned, encrypted, portable intelligence that is not trapped inside one AI company.

The Twin is the sovereign user context. Sivraj is the system that creates, protects, updates, retrieves from, and exposes that Twin to approved AI systems.

Category line:

> Persistent intelligence for the AI era.

Use this in business, investor, enterprise, API, and infrastructure contexts. It names the category Sivraj wants to own.

Preferred copy:

> Sivraj builds and protects your sovereign AI Twin.

Shorter product copy:

> Sivraj is where your Twin lives.

Do not describe Sivraj as a chatbot, notes app, document vault, or vector database.

### Name Origin

Sivraj is Jarvis backwards.

Do not lead with this in core positioning. Use it only when explaining the name or in about-page storytelling.

About-page copy:

> We named it Sivraj because it is Jarvis, the AI assistant everyone wanted, but reversed. This time, the AI works for you. Not the platform. Not us. You.

### Taglines

Primary:

> Your memory. Your intelligence. Yours.

Secondary:

> The AI that remembers what matters.

Trust-forward:

> Built around you. Controlled by you.

Name-origin line:

> Your JARVIS. Backwards and forwards.

Avoid using "forever" as the default tagline until persistence, deletion, account recovery, export, and long-term storage semantics are fully productized and legally reviewed.

### Positioning Statement

Sivraj builds your sovereign AI Twin: a private, encrypted intelligence layer that remembers your work, recovers forgotten context, and helps every AI system you use understand you with permission.

Unlike ordinary AI tools, Sivraj is designed around continuity. Your Twin does not reset with every app, model, or session. It compounds from your uploaded context, processed memories, retrieval history, graph, goals, and permissions.

### Voice and Tone

Sivraj should sound:

- Calm.
- Personal.
- Confident.
- Direct.
- Serious about trust.

Sivraj should not sound:

- Crypto-bro.
- Hype-driven.
- Startup-cute.
- Overly technical in consumer copy.
- Like generic AI productivity software.

Consumer copy should avoid leading with terms like Web3, blockchain, decentralized, vector database, embeddings, or object storage. Technical docs should still explicitly mention Sui, Walrus, Seal, encryption, storage proofs, policy, and permission boundaries.

### Trust Language

Use precise trust language:

- Private memory is encrypted before durable storage.
- Raw private memory is stored as ciphertext through Walrus.
- Postgres stores metadata, processing state, audit records, graph/index records, and raw storage references.
- Access is scoped by authentication, permissions, and Seal/Sui policy.
- Walrus-backed references make memory more portable and verifiable.

Avoid absolute claims unless they are technically and legally guaranteed:

- Avoid: "Sivraj can never delete it."
- Prefer: "Sivraj stores encrypted memory references designed for durable, portable access."
- Avoid: "We cannot sell your data."
- Prefer: "We do not sell your data; private memory is encrypted before storage and access is governed by explicit permission."
- Avoid: "No platform can take it from you."
- Prefer: "Your Twin is designed to survive across models, apps, and agents instead of being trapped in one AI ecosystem."

## First User Persona

### Category

The first wedge is high-leverage independent operators.

This includes independent consultants, founders, solo builders, researchers, creators, and expert knowledge workers whose advantage comes from compounding personal and professional context.

Founder OS remains an important expansion path, but the first persona should prove personal sovereign intelligence before team/company intelligence.

### Primary Persona: Tunde

Tunde is a 34-year-old independent strategy or tech consultant working across Lagos and London.

He works across multiple clients, writes proposals, builds frameworks, uses AI daily, and accumulates expertise over years. His knowledge is his main asset, but it is scattered across Google Drive, Notion, PDFs, old client notes, ChatGPT threads, and email.

His pain:

> I know I solved this before. I just cannot find where I put it.

He is not disorganized. He is under-infrastructured.

Current workflow:

```text
Client brief -> Google + ChatGPT -> start from scratch -> save note somewhere -> forget it exists -> repeat
```

He pays because Sivraj helps him:

- Recover work he already did.
- Reuse forgotten frameworks and insight.
- Prepare faster for client conversations.
- Compound his expertise instead of restarting from scratch.
- Build a professional moat around his own memory and judgment.

What he tells a friend:

> It is like having an assistant who has read everything I have ever written and actually remembers the useful parts.

## Activation Event

The activation event is the moment the user asks a real work question and Sivraj returns something specific from their own past that they forgot they knew.

Example:

> In March 2024 you worked through a similar positioning problem for a fintech client. You concluded that leading with compliance reduced procurement friction. Here is the framework you built then.

This is the core product moment.

It works because it is:

- Personal.
- Specific.
- Source-backed.
- Impossible for cold-start ChatGPT to reproduce.
- Emotionally obvious without requiring a technical explanation.

Activation target:

> Under 12 minutes from signup to first personally useful remembered insight.

Minimum activation path:

1. User connects wallet and creates a Twin.
2. User uploads a small but meaningful context set.
3. Sivraj encrypts and stores private raw memory through Seal/Walrus.
4. Worker processes artifacts into memory fragments.
5. User asks a real work question.
6. Sivraj retrieves source-backed personal context.
7. User sees a specific memory, timestamp, source, and storage proof.

## Insight Quality Rubric

Core test:

> Would the user pay to receive this insight?

Good Sivraj insights are specific, sourced, actionable, surprising, timely, and only meaningful for this user's history.

Insight tiers:

- **Tier 1: Retrieval** - finds something the user stored and forgot.
- **Tier 2: Connection** - links two things the user never consciously connected.
- **Tier 3: Pattern** - identifies a behavioral or strategic pattern across time.
- **Tier 4: Anticipation** - predicts friction or opportunity before the user asks.

Disqualify insights that are:

- Generic life advice.
- Unsourced assertions.
- Restatements of what the user just said.
- Cold-start ChatGPT answers.
- Positive platitudes.
- Personality claims without evidence.

Every serious insight should cite the source memories that support it.

## Demo Strategy

### Current Demo

The current product can demonstrate:

- Sui wallet sign-in.
- Encrypted manual memory upload.
- Text, Markdown, and PDF upload.
- Walrus encrypted storage receipt.
- Worker processing.
- Memory fragment creation.
- Basic retrieval over processed memory.
- Database and audit verification.

This demo should not claim live graph construction or advanced pattern synthesis yet.

### Target Demo Narrative

The target demo should show:

1. Upload life/work context.
2. Build the Twin's graph and memory model.
3. Ask a real work question.
4. Receive a source-backed remembered insight.
5. Show encrypted storage proof and scoped access.
6. Route different context to different AI agents.

Target wow moment:

> He did not remember writing that. His Twin did.

## Pricing Test Plan

Principle:

> Do not price storage. Price recovered expertise, faster execution, and compounding intelligence.

Initial pricing hypotheses:

- **Free**: basic Twin, limited uploads/queries, activation-focused.
- **Pro**: around $29/month for independent operators like Tunde.
- **Studio**: around $79/month for small teams, agencies, and shared client spaces.
- **Founder Intelligence**: premium packaging for founder/operator workflows after synthesis and reporting mature.

Future research:

- Legacy preservation.
- Estate access.
- Family sharing.
- Permanent Twin preservation.

These require separate security, recovery, legal, and long-term storage design before becoming product promises.

Pricing red lines:

- Do not charge per query.
- Do not monetize raw storage, embeddings, or uploads as the main value.
- Do not gate the activation moment before Sivraj proves value.
- Do not sell "Web3 storage" as the user-facing product.

Success metric:

> 40% of users who reach the activation moment convert to Pro within 7 days.
